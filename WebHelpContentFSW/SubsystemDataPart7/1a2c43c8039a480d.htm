Results to check the power supply characteristics of the amplifier

These results are available when you turn on the baseband input.

For valid results, make sure that you have configured the measurement correctly regarding the equipment you are using (see "Configuring PAE measurements (Power Added Efficiency)").

Baseband Input Voltage I

Voltage measured at the I channel of the analyzer baseband input.

FETCh:​IVOLtage:​PURE:​CURRent[:​RESult]?

Baseband Input Voltage Q

Voltage measured at the Q channel of the analyzer baseband input.

FETCh:​QVOLtage:​PURE:​CURRent[:​RESult]?

Voltage

Voltage measured at the Q channel of the analyzer baseband input.

This value represents the supply voltage of the power amplifier.

The result is the same as the "Baseband Input Voltage Q" when the multiplier = 1 and the offset = 0 (see "Configuring PAE measurements (Power Added Efficiency)")

FETCh:​VCC:​CURRent[:​RESult]?

Current

Current measured at the I channel of the baseband input. This corresponds to the current drawn by the amplifier.

FETCh:​ICC:​CURRent[:​RESult]?

Power

DC power measured at the baseband input. The DC power is the product of the measured voltage and current.

FETCh:​BBPower:​CURRent[:​RESult]?

Average PAE

The average Power Added Efficiency (PAE) indicates the efficiency of the amplifier.

The PAE is the ratio of the difference between RF output and input power and the DC power:

PAE = (Output Power - Input Power) / DC power
