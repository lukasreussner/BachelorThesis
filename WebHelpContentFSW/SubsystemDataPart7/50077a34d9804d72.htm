Pulse Duty Cycle

Defines the duty cycle of a pulsed reference signal.

The duty cycle of a pulse is the ratio of the pulse duration and the actual length of the pulse. A duty cycle of 100 % corresponds to a continuous signal.

Example:

The pulse duration is 2 μs. The actual length of the pulse is 1 μs. The duty cycle is 1 μs : 2 μs = 0.5 or 50 %.
