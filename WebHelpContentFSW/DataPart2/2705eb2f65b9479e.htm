Measurement Example – Measuring the APD and CCDF of White Noise Generated by the R&S FSW
Setting the RBW

When the amplitude distribution is measured, the analysis bandwidth must be set so that the complete spectrum of the signal to be measured falls within the bandwidth. This is the only way of ensuring that all the amplitudes will pass through the IF filter without being distorted. If the selected bandwidth is too small for a digitally modulated signal, the amplitude distribution at the output of the IF filter becomes a Gaussian distribution according to the central limit theorem and thus corresponds to a white noise signal. The true amplitude distribution of the signal therefore cannot be determined.

A programming example demonstrating a statistics measurement in a remote environment is provided in "Programming Example: Measuring Statistics".

  1. Preset the R&S FSW.

  2. Set the reference level to -60 dBm.

    The R&S FSW's intrinsic noise is displayed at the top of the screen.

  3. Select the "APD" measurement function from the "Select Measurement" dialog box.

    The R&S FSW sets the frequency span to 0 Hz and measures the amplitude probability distribution (APD). The number of uncorrelated level measurements used for the measurement is 100000. The mean power and the peak power are displayed in dBm. The crest factor (peak power – mean power) is output as well.

    Amplitude probability distribution of white noise

  4. Now select the "CCDF" measurement function from the "Select Measurement" dialog box.

    CCDF of white noise

    The CCDF trace indicates the probability that a level will exceed the mean power. The level above the mean power is plotted along the x-axis of the graph. The origin of the axis corresponds to the mean power level. The probability that a level will be exceeded is plotted along the y-axis.
