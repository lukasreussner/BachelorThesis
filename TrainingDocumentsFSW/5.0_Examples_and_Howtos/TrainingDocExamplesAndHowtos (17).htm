Measuring the Edges of a GSM Burst with High Time Resolution

Due to the high time resolution of the R&S FSW at the 0 Hz display range, the edges of TDMA bursts can be measured precisely. The edges can be shifted to the display area using the trigger offset.

Test setup
Signal generator settings (e.g. R&S SMW)

Frequency

890 MHz

Level

0 dBm

Modulation

GSM, one timeslot activated

The measurement is based on the example "Measuring the Power of a GSM Burst During the Activation Phase".

  1. Switch off the power measurement.

    1. Press the MEAS key.

    2. Select "All Functions Off".

  2. Increase the time resolution to 100 µs (SWEEP > "Sweep Time Manual").

  3. Shift the rising edge of the GSM burst to the center of the display by defining a trigger offset.

    1. Press the TRIG key.

    2. Select "Trigger Offset".

    3. By turning the rotary knob counterclockwise, reduce the trigger offset until the burst edge is displayed in the center of the display, or enter -50 µs.

      The R&S FSW displays the rising edge of the GSM burst.

      Rising edge of the GSM burst displayed with high time resolution

  4. Move the falling edge of the burst to the center of the display. To do so, switch the trigger "Slope" to "Falling" (TRIG > "Trigger/ Gate Config").

    The R&S FSW displays the falling edge of the GSM burst.

    Falling edge of the GSM burst displayed with high time resolution
