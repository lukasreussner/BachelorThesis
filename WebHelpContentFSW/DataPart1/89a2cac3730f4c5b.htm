Gap Channel Spacing

Gap channel spacings are normally predefined by the MSR standard but can be changed.

Gap channels are defined using bandwidths and spacings, relative to the outer edges of the surrounding sub blocks. Since the upper and lower gap channels are identical, only two channels must be configured. The required spacing can be determined according to the following formula (indicated for lower channels):

Spacing = [CF of the gap channel] - [left sub block center] + ([RF bandwidth of left sub block] /2)

(See also Figure "Sub block definition" and Figure "Gap channel definition for lower gap".)

For details, see "How to Configure an MSR ACLR Measurement".
