Modulation Errors

A 2FSK signal is generated using a GMSK frequency pulse. Examples of carrier drift and reference deviation are shown in Figure "The reference and distorted instantaneous frequency of a GMSK signal with a carrier frequency drift" and Figure "The reference and measured instantaneous frequency of a GMSK signal with reference deviation error", respectively.

Carrier frequency drift

A carrier frequency drift is modeled as a linear change in the carrier frequency over time. The effect of carrier drift on the instantaneous frequency of an FSK signal is illustrated in Figure "The reference and distorted instantaneous frequency of a GMSK signal with a carrier frequency drift".

FSK deviation error

The FSK deviation error is the difference between the measured frequency deviation and the user-defined reference frequency deviation (see "FSK Ref Deviation (FSK only)"). The evidence of a deviation error in the instantaneous frequency of an FSK signal is demonstrated in Figure "The reference and measured instantaneous frequency of a GMSK signal with reference deviation error".
