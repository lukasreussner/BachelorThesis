Horizontal Line

Displays an absolute or relative horizontal line in an eye diagram (see "Result Types in VSA"). A relative line is only available if an absolute horizontal line is already displayed.

The position of the lines can be defined numerically or by dragging the line to a new position in the diagram.

The absolute y-value of the eye diagram at the position of the first line is indicated in the diagram. The relative y-value of the eye diagram at the position of the second line, in relation to the y-value at the first line, is also indicated. The unit of the vertical lines depends on the unit of the y-axis in the diagram.

If you position the lines at the top and the bottom of the eye, the relative line value represents the height of the eye.

See also "How to Measure the Size of an Eye".

Remote command: 

CALCulate<n>:​DLABs:​STATe

CALCulate<n>:​DLABs[:​VALue]

CALCulate<n>:​DLRel:​STATe
